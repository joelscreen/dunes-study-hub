The National Identity Mark (NIM) in the UAE is an annual inspection and evaluation framework launched in 2023 by the Abu Dhabi Department of Education and Knowledge (ADEK). Its purpose is to evaluate how effectively private schools in Abu Dhabi foster a deep-rooted understanding of Emirati culture, values, and citizenship among students. National Identity - ADEK National Identity - ADEK The framework serves to empower parents to make informed decisions and ensures that national identity is embedded within school curricula, strengthening the connection between students and the UAE’s traditions.   1. Domain: Culture
    2. This domain focuses on connecting students with the nation's roots, history, and language.
    1.1. Element: Arabic Language – Evaluates how Arabic is taught, promoted, and used as a language of communication within the school.
    1.2. Element: History – Assesses how the UAE’s history is taught, including the nation's foundations and major milestones.
    1.3. Element: Heritage – Focuses on connecting the nation’s past to its present through, for example, traditional activities, clothing, and arts.

  2. Domain: Values
    3. This domain focuses on promoting the moral and social principles that define the UAE community, inspired by the late Sheikh Zayed bin Sultan Al Nahyan.
    2.1. Element: Respect – Evaluates the promotion of mutual understanding, harmony, and tolerance within the school community.
    2.2. Element: Compassion – Assesses the emphasis placed on the wellbeing of society members, empathy, and unity.
    2.3. Element: Global Understanding – Focuses on helping students understand the UAE’s role in international relations and its engagement with the world.

  3. Domain: Citizenship
    4. This domain focuses on cultivating a sense of belonging, responsibility, and active participation in society.
    3.1. Element: Belonging – Promotes a sense of pride in national identity and connection to the UAE.
    3.2. Element: Volunteering – Encourages active civic participation and altruism.
    3.3. Element: Conservation – Evaluates how schools encourage sustainability, eco-friendly practices, and the preservation of natural resources.
    Key Aspects of the NIM Evaluation
  • Independent Evaluation: The NIM is assessed separately from the annual Irtiqa'a inspections, which cover general school performance.
  • 4-Tiered Rating System: Schools are ranked as Outstanding, Good, Acceptable, or Weak based on their performance across the three domains.
  • Targeted Schools: While initially targeting schools with high Emirati enrollment, the framework encourages all private schools to integrate these elements.
  • Methodology: Evaluators use a mix of school visits, lesson observations, review of documents, and direct interaction with students, teachers, and staff to evaluate the impact on school culture.
