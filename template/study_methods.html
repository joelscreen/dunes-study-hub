STUDY TRICKS DOCUMENT:-


This document explains proven study tricks, why they work, and how to use them in real life. Following all these methods regularly can ensure drastic improvement in your daily work.
1. FLOW STATE
What it is:
This is one of the most crucial parts in work, and it involves activating a secret state of your brain called "Flow State". When you're in flow state, work feels effortless,
concepts start clicking, and you become a completely different person. Why it works:
• Your brain starts enjoying the task, and you start completing really hard tasks with no effort at all
How to use it:
• Flow state requires clarity. Instead of saying, I'll study math for 20 minutes, say, I'll complete 10 sums, create my own questions, and connect the topics to my daily life.
• Perform a ritual (like breathing 3 times, humming a tune, or anything) before doing any kind of work.
• Do tasks that are at least 4% harder than your sweet spot. This helps the brain enjoy while pushing its regular limits, unlocking flow state.
• Force yourself to be locked into the task. The beginning of flow state will be hard, but that's what enforces this system.
• Don't check the time. Flow kicks off 10-15 minutes later, but checking the time diverts your attention and prolongs flow even further.
2. ACTIVE RECALL
What it is:
Active recall means forcing your brain to pull information out, instead of just rereading or highlighting. Active recall can be performed in the form of blurting,
feynman method, flashcards, etc.
Why it works:
• Your brain strengthens memories when it retrieves them. Rereading feels productive, but it mostly trains recognition, not memory.
How to use it:
• Read the source carefully
• Close the book or chrome tab, and force your brain to pull out the information, either orally or by writing
• Go back and check which parts you messed up
• Use spaced repetition to find out what days you must active recall
3. SPACE REPETITION
What it is:
Spaced repetition means reviewing information over increasing time gaps right before you forget it. Research shows that the below time intervals are the most optimal for locking
in information for 3-4 months in just 10-14 days.
Why it works
Your brain forgets things over time. Reviewing just before forgetting locks the memory in longer.
How to use it
Instead of studying math formulas for 3 hours in one day:
• Study: Learn formula
• 1 day later: Quick review
• 3 days later: Another review
• 7 days later: Final check
• (optional, to lock the info for longer periods of time) 14 days later: Answer questions from the textbook or an external source
• (continue to double the recall time to lock the info even longer)
4. THE FEYNMAN TECHNIQUE
What it is:
Learning by teaching the concept as simply as possible.
Why it works
If you can explain something simply, you truly understand it. Gaps become obvious immediately.
How to use it:
• If you're studying electricity, you can recall like this:
“Electricity is like water flowing through pipes. Voltage is pressure, current is...”
• If you get stuck explaining, that's your weak spot.
• Try to force in the information as hard as you can.
• If it doesn't click however hard you try, skip that and continue
• Later, go back to the textbook and ONLY see that part.
• Explain the concept as if you're teaching a 5-year old, or go up to a mirror and explain it to yourself
4. POMODORO
What it is:
Studying in short, focused sessions with breaks. The breaks are used by the brain to pass down the information into the long-term memory, just like what it does while sleeping.
Classic structure
• 25 minutes study
• 5 minutes break
• After 4 times, long break (15 minutes)
Why it works
• Prevents burnout
• Reduces procrastination
• Trains focus like a muscle
How to use it:
• Go to the pomodoro section in this app
• Start the pomodoro
• During the pomodoro, focus on studying only
• If you stay dedicated, you might enter flow state during the focus period
• During the 5 minutes break, take a short nap, stare at a wall, or just walk. Let your mind wander. When you're doing nothing, your brain starts working and stores the information more efficiently.
• The breaks help the brain reset and prepare for the next study session.
• Even a small peak at your phone can disrupt the next 4 pomodoros, so only use your device after you complete 4-5 pomodoros.
5. THETA WAVES
What it is:
A particular noise that enforces the secret "ultra-focus" part of the brain.
Why it works:
• Your brain responds to different music in different ways.
• Theta waves makes the brain transmit information in neurons much faster.
How to use it:
• Search "Theta waves" or "White noise" in YouTube.
• Play the sound in the background.
• Make sure to make the sound not so quiet that you won't hear it at all, and not so loud to distract you.
5. INTERLEAVING
What it is:
Mixing different topics or problem types in one study session.
Why it works
• It forces :your brain to choose the right strategy, instead of running on autopilot.
How to use it:
• Start a subject, like physics.
• After you complete your goal, do something similar, like chemistry, as it falls under the same category.
• This helps your brain reset faster.
6. BLURTING METHOD
What it is:
Study → close everything → write down everything you remember.
Why it works:
• Combines active recall + gap detection
• Makes weak areas painfully obvious (which is good)
How to use it:
• Read your notes or a source for about 5-10 minutes
• Close notes
• Write everything you remember about what you just read
• Compare your answer with the actual answer
• Underline the words or phrases you made wrong
• Write the answer again, without looking at your original answer
• 2 blurts per pomodoro is the most ideal
7. VISUALIZATION RECALL
What it is:
Learning using words + visuals together.
Why it works:
• The human brain is designed to recognize patterns. Drawing images or flowcharts can force the brain to connect the dots, further enforcing the information
Real-life example:
• Drawing diagrams for physics
• Mind maps for biology
• Drawing minifigures as kings or presidents for history
• Using arrow marks + formulas for math
8. AREA NOTES RECALL
What it is:
Placing small sticky notes all around the house, especially where you visit the most (fridge, TV, phone, bedroom)
Why it works:
• When you see some concepts over and over, your brain labels that as "important" and stores it in the long term memory
How to use it:
• Take a date or a formula that you want to learn
• Write it on a sticky note
• Stick it in places you visit the most
• The next time you see the note, force yourself to visualize it before doing anything else
9. ENVIRONMENTAL CONTROL
What it is:
Optimizing your surroundings so studying requires less willpower.
Why it works:
Discipline is limited. Environment beats motivation.
Real-life example
• Phone in another room
• Different study area every day
• Study-only playlist
9. SLEEP & BREAKS
Why it matters:
• Memory consolidation happens during sleep. No sleep results in weak learning.
Real-life example:
• Studying 2 hours + sleeping well > studying 5 hours exhausted
10. METACOGNITION
What it is:
Knowing what you know and what you don't.
Why it works:
Metacognition prevents false confidence, helping you get a better understanding about your knowledge in that particular field.
How to do it:
After studying, ask:
• Can I explain this without notes?
• Can I solve a new problem?
11. Cues
What it is:
This method is mostly used for remembering small tasks, but can be implimented into studies. It requires you to set a cue for a certain topic or formula to remember it later.
Why it works:
The brain remembers images and actions more than words. Cues act as hints, which the brain can use to remember the information you assigned to it.
How to do it:
• Decide which small information to remember (small things like formulas, dates, etc. big things like paragraphs cannot be assigned to a cue)
• Think of a cue (like snapping your fingers, looking at a certain image, the list goes on...)
• Perform the cue while focusing deeply on the information. You may say it aloud as well.
• Now, at regular intervals of the day (not immediately), perform the cue, in order to lock the information harder.
12. The 2-minute method
What it is:
Naturally, the brain tries to avoid studying or work, especially if it is held for long periods of time. The 2-minute method tricks the brain into
underestimating the study time.
Why it works:
Studying reduces dopamine for most people, so when you trick your brain into studying for short periods, it gets interested, as less dopamine is lost.
How to do it:
• Start a 2 minute timer
• Do the smallest task possible, example:- Instead of studying chemistry, you can read a small paragraph or recall any formulas
• After the 2 minute timer ends, you can stop, but most people would generally continue
• If you feel the urge to complete what you were doing, exceeding the 2 minute timer, then method worked for you

By applying all these scientifically proven methods, your brain will have a better time, recalling, understanding, and reviewing information.
